Odessa

Odessa is a port city on the Black Sea in southern Ukraine. It is known for its beaches and 19th century architecture, for example, the building of the Odessa Opera and Ballet Theater. The Potemkin Stairs, which gained worldwide fame thanks to the film Battleship Potemkin, leads to the sea, where Vorontsov lighthouse is located. Along the coast stretches Primorsky Boulevard. Here you can walk and admire the coastal mansions and monuments.Read more...

Lviv

Lviv is a city in the west of Ukraine, about 70 kilometers from the border with Poland. The architecture of the city keeps traces of Polish and Austro-Hungarian heritage and combines the styles of Eastern and Central Europe, namely Italy and Germany. In the park of the High Castle on the top of the mountain, the ruins of a 14th century fortress are preserved. From here there is a panoramic view of the green domes of the churches and the hills surrounding the city.Read more...

Kiev

Kiev is the capital of Ukraine, located on the Dnieper River. The city is famous for its monuments of religious architecture and historical museums. The Kiev-Pechersk Lavra of the 11th century is a monastery and a center of pilgrimage. On its territory there are several churches topped with golden domes. This architectural complex is famous for its underground passages leading to the tombs of Orthodox monks, and a collection of gold objects from Scythian times.Read more...
